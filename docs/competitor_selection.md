# Competitor Selection for BCBA Mock Exam GEO

This project should not treat every similar website as a benchmark competitor.
For GEO optimization, a useful competitor is a site that is both relevant and
worth learning from.

## Competitor Tiers

### Tier 1: Direct Product Competitors

Sites that compete for users looking for BCBA mock exams, practice exams,
question banks, and exam-prep practice questions.

Use these for:

- page structure comparisons
- pricing and offer comparisons
- FAQ gap analysis
- mock-exam feature comparisons
- AI Visibility Share of Voice

### Tier 2: Authority / Broad Exam Prep Competitors

Larger BCBA exam-prep brands with stronger content ecosystems, communities,
courses, or brand recognition.

Use these for:

- content strategy inspiration
- trust-signal benchmarking
- topical authority analysis
- AI answer monitoring

### Tier 3: Informational Competitors

Blogs, review pages, resource hubs, and content sites that may rank or appear
in AI answers for informational queries.

Use these for:

- article structure
- internal linking ideas
- question and topic expansion

## What Makes A Competitor Worth Learning From

Score each candidate from 0-3 on each criterion.

| Criterion | What To Check |
|---|---|
| Business relevance | Does the site sell or promote BCBA mock exams, exam prep, practice questions, or study tools? |
| Search visibility | Does the site appear for non-branded BCBA exam prep / mock exam queries? |
| Brand visibility | Is the brand commonly mentioned in Reddit, forums, AI answers, or exam-prep discussions? |
| Content depth | Does the site cover exam format, study plans, domains, ethics, measurement, mock questions, and pass strategy? |
| Trust signals | Does it show authors, credentials, testimonials, reviews, refund terms, update dates, or transparent methodology? |
| Conversion structure | Are offers, CTAs, pricing, guarantees, sample questions, and course paths clear? |
| GEO suitability | Does the content include direct answers, FAQ, tables, steps, and quotable explanations? |

## Traffic And CTR Caveat

Competitor traffic and click-through rate cannot be known exactly from outside.

- Exact CTR is only available to the site owner through tools such as Google
  Search Console or paid ad platforms.
- External tools such as Similarweb, Semrush, Ahrefs, and SE Ranking provide
  estimates, not ground truth.
- For this project, use traffic estimates as a prioritization signal, not as an
  absolute fact.

## How We Should Use These Competitors

1. Use Tier 1 competitors to compare product pages and conversion flows.
2. Use Tier 2 competitors to learn content depth, trust signals, and topical authority.
3. Use AI Visibility monitoring to see which competitors are actually mentioned
   by AI answers for our target prompts.
4. Re-rank the competitor list after we collect real prompt-level visibility data.
